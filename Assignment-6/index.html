Dak Bulgogi

___________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________

Dak Bulgogi is a Korean dish that is made with chicken, vegetables, and a spicy sauce. It is a very popular dish in Korea
and is often served with rice. It is also a very easy dish to make and is a great way to introduce yourself to Korean cuisine.

Ingredients

____________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________

  • 8 boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts if preferred
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tbs minced garlic
  • 3 tbs sesame oil toasted or non toasted chef's choice
  • 1 tbs peeled and grated fresh ginger
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tbs rice vinegar

____________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________


Directions

____________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________________

  1. Slice the Chicken into thin, even pieces (1/2 in thick), and remove any excess fat
    • If you are having trouble cutting it evenly, freeze the meat for 30 minutes first
    • Make sure to also cut against the grain
    • If you don't understand what against the grain means, check this video out
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together all the spices with the Sesame Oil.
  3. Whisk again for fun now. (its important you enjoy making the food)
  4. Once done, add the chicken pieces into the large bowl, and cover it carefully.
  5. Now shake it, like a bully shakes a kid down for their lunch money.
  6. This step has a choice for you to do
    • For best results stick in the fridge for 24 hours
    • If you are on a time crunch, stick it in the fridge for 4 hours.
  7. After chosen marination time has elapsed, take the chicken out, and shake off any excess marinade.
  8. Turn on either tabletop grill, or outside grill to a medium heat.
  9. Once heated, place chicken on grill, and cook around 10 minutes on each side.
  10. Do not move the chicken around much while its grilling so you can get that nice browning on each side.
    • Make sure the juice from the chicken runs clear, as well as having no pink in the middle.
  11. Once done, plate the Bulgogi, and Enjoy!
